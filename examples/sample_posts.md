## 1. Problem → Solution · LinkedIn · Plumbing

**Title (internal):** Shared inbox chaos on payroll morning  

Your office manager hits the office at 6:45.

Payroll’s due. Three techs already texted about parts. And the shared company Gmail just… won’t log in.

Nobody knows who changed the password last. Or if it was a “security prompt” that wasn’t real.

Here’s the boring fix that works for shops our size:

1. One business account owner (not five).
2. MFA on every login that can see customer data or money.
3. A password manager — not a sticky note on the dispatch monitor.
4. When someone leaves, offboard them the same day you collect the truck keys.

IT doesn’t have to be fancy. It has to be *owned*.

If your plumbing shop is still running on shared logins, that’s a one-afternoon project — not a six-month roadmap.

## 2. Educational / How-To · Facebook · HVAC

**Title:** MFA in plain English  

MFA is not “extra hassle.”

It’s the second lock on the shop door.

Password = key.  
Phone prompt / authenticator app = you proving it’s actually you.

For an HVAC company: turn it on for email first. That’s where invoices, vendor scams, and “updated W-9” junk land.

Takes most shops under an hour. Beats explaining to a customer why someone emailed them from your domain asking for a wire transfer.

Questions? I’m in the East Valley — happy to walk through it without the jargon.

## 4. Opinion / Contrarian · LinkedIn · Construction / GC

**Title:** Cousin IT is not a plan  

Unpopular but true:

Your cousin who “knows computers” is a gift for printer problems.

He is not a security plan.

Trade shops get burned the same way every year:

- Shared passwords  
- No offboarding  
- Backups that haven’t been tested since Obama was in office  
- Insurance questionnaire filled out with guesses  

You wouldn’t let a helper design your load calculations. Don’t let goodwill design your access control.

Start smaller than you think:

MFA.  
Known backups.  
One person who owns IT decisions.

Month-to-month help beats a 36-month contract written in a language nobody on the job site speaks.

That’s the hill I’ll die on.

Your trucks are insured. Your conversations should be too.

## 6. Educational · LinkedIn · All trades

**Title:** Cyber insurance questions that trip contractors  

Brokers keep asking trade owners the same five things:

1. Do you use MFA on email?  
2. Who has admin rights?  
3. Are backups offline or immutable somehow?  
4. Do you train people on phishing?  
5. What happens when an employee leaves?

If you can’t answer without calling three people… the questionnaire isn’t the problem. Visibility is.

A Cyber Risk Snapshot is just a structured way to answer those in English — useful for GCs and carriers, not just “IT people.”

No scare tactics. Just fewer blank stares at renewal time.
